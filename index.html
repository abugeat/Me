Développement d’une méthode de radiosité pour l’étude du rayonnement solaire en milieu urbain

This PhD thesis introduces an innovative simulation method for solar radiation in dense urban areas, enhancing accuracy by incorporating both diffuse and specular reflections through extended radiosity and view factors. The study demonstrates practical applications, addressing daylight scarcity through interventions like light wells and high-diffuse-reflectance materials. It also explores the impact of surface properties on solar gain in urban settings, emphasizing the method's efficiency in architectural design contexts.

I applied rendering techniques like radiosity, ray-tracing, and path-tracing to architecture and building engineering. I collaborated in a diverse, international team spanning France, Spain, Uruguay, and Belgium, involving expertise from Architecture, Physics, and Computer Science. Additionally, I enhanced my skills in code development and computer science during a two-month research fellowship at UdelaR's Computer Science Department.
